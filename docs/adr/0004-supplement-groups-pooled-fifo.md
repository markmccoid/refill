# Groups pool interchangeable brands into one sequential FIFO queue

A **Group** links two or more Supplements (brands) that fill the same role — e.g. "Fish Oil" spanning two brands — into a single unit consumed **one brand at a time in sequence**. The Group owns the combined run-out forecast, cost roll-up, per-person dosage, and the anchor clock; its members stay full Supplement entries (each keeps its own supplement-facts, image, and priced bottle ledger — the reason brands are separate in the first place).

Consumption drains **one pooled FIFO queue of every bottle across the member brands, oldest-purchase-first**, with the currently-open bottle pinned to the front and a manual override for when real-world intent differs from purchase dates. Only the open bottle depletes; **sealed brands sit frozen** until the queue rolls to them. This is ADR-0002's per-supplement FIFO drain lifted one level — a solo supplement is just the degenerate one-member walk.

This **refines ADR-0001 and ADR-0002, it does not replace them.** No cron, on-hand computed at read time, and the re-anchor invariant all survive — but re-anchor now operates at the **Group** level: any change to any member (add/remove/recount a bottle, edit a dosage, add/remove a taker, link/unlink a brand, or reorder the queue) snapshots *every* member bottle's current remaining and resets the one shared clock to now.

## Considered Options

- **Group as a first-class entity (chosen)** — owns the sequence and the single "currently-open brand." Only a real owner can express "finish the open Brand A bottle, then Brand B, then the second Brand A bottle."
- **Group as a cosmetic tag/filter on supplements** — rejected: a label can't express one-brand-at-a-time consumption or a single pooled forecast; brands would keep depleting independently.
- **Group-of-one (every supplement always in a group, uniform code path)** — rejected: forces the concept onto every solo entry and requires migrating all existing supplements into auto-groups. Chose an **optional overlay** instead; solo supplements are unchanged and the group forecast is the same walk with one member.
- **Pure purchase-date FIFO with no override** — rejected: usually right (it's what makes the motivating scenario work for free), but can't handle a sealed older bottle you deliberately want to consume after a newer already-open one. Kept date-FIFO as the **default** with a manual override.
- **Dosage per brand (always)** vs **dosage on the group (always)** — chose a **hybrid**: dosage is set once on the Group and inherited by every brand, with an optional **per-brand override** for the rare half-strength brand. Override is **deferred** — shipped as a nullable field; the piecewise-rate forecast it requires is only built when the first override appears.

## Consequences

- **The forecast is a division only while no override exists.** With group dosage the rate is constant, so run-out stays `onHand ÷ rate`. A per-brand override makes the group's rate **piecewise** — an iterative walk over the queue applying each bottle's owning-brand rate. This is the deliberately-deferred complexity; it lands in the rate resolver, not a migration.
- **A member brand's individual run-out is meaningless in isolation** (sealed brands freeze), which is exactly why the dashboard collapses a Group to **one row** — combined on-hand + a single forecast — expandable to the member brands, which one is open, and each one's remaining.
- **Re-anchor is all-or-nothing per group.** You can no longer re-anchor one member alone; recounting Brand B re-anchors Brand A too, because they share one clock and one pooled history.
- **Cost pools with no new concept.** Spend rate = consumption rate × the *open bottle's* cost-per-pill (the open bottle simply belongs to whichever brand is open, so spend **steps** at a brand rollover, not just a bottle boundary); lifetime spent sums every member bottle's price.
- **Group identity:** user-set name (the role — no brand carries it), optional category, and a dashboard image borrowed from the **currently-open brand** (so it changes at each rollover).
- **A Group holds ≥2 members:** it forms by linking two brands and **auto-dissolves** back to a solo Supplement when unlinked down to one — no one-member groups, no pre-staging.
- **Group dosage is stored by materialisation, not a new table.** Rather than add a group-scoped dosage table, the group's per-person dosage is written identically onto every member's existing `dosages` rows. So all members share one rate (constant group rate) and every existing per-supplement path — rate, re-anchor, cost — works unchanged; a future per-brand override is just "let one member's rows differ." The visible oddity is duplicate-looking dosage rows across a group's members, and editing a grouped member's dosage directly would desync the group (so the member detail page routes dosage edits to the group).
- **Link-time dosage is resolved explicitly, not merged.** When brands that each already carry per-person dosages are grouped, the create-group step pre-fills the group dosage from the currently-open brand and asks the user to confirm; the members' prior per-brand values are **discarded**, not turned into overrides. This keeps a freshly-created group override-free (constant rate, simple `onHand ÷ rate` forecast) — a genuinely differing brand gets an override added deliberately afterward.
