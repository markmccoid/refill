# Restock Planner: manual prices, user-curated plan, first-class Retailer

The Restock Planner (replacing the placeholder Buy page) turns run-out forecasts into retailer purchases: a single persisted **Restock Plan** per household whose items carry manually entered prices, a selected **Offer** (brand + Retailer chosen together — a Group item is fulfilled by one member brand), and a planned quantity; a derived **Retailer order** view groups selections with free-shipping-threshold status; **Mark as Purchased** (per retailer order, with an actuals-confirmation step) lands each unit as an individual bottle row through the existing `bottles.add` re-anchor path. Three deliberate reversals of the previously documented direction are recorded here because a future reader will find the old plan (`FUTURE_FEATURES.md`'s scraping-based price finder) and wonder why the shipped feature does the opposite.

## Considered Options

- **Manual price entry (chosen) vs automated scraping.** The original Buy plan scraped retailer prices into a `priceQuotes` table (sticker, shipping, stock, ETA). Rejected: scrapers are fragile against retailer markup changes and bot defenses, and — decisively — they can't see the price that matters, which is account-specific (member pricing, subscribe-and-save, logged-in coupons). Instead the planner opens the saved link in a new tab ("Check Site ↗") and the user types the price they actually see. `priceQuotes` is dropped with the scraping plan.
- **User-curated plan membership (chosen) vs forecast auto-population.** Auto-adding forecast-qualifying items was designed first and rejected: it forces a dismissal state machine (dismissed status, expiry on forecast-change or window-elapse) just so users can fight rows they didn't ask for. Instead the plan contains only what the user picked from a run-out-ordered checkbox modal; the forecast informs but never acts. The safety net moves to a sidebar badge (count of supplements running out within the **forecast window**, default 30 days, that aren't on the plan).
- **First-class household Retailer entity (chosen) vs deriving retailers from bottle URL domains.** Saved purchase links per supplement↔retailer, average past price per retailer, free-shipping thresholds, and retailers never yet purchased from all need an entity to hang on; URL-domain grouping can express none of them. Bottles gain an optional `retailerId`, kept in step with their purchase URL **at logging time**: whenever a bottle is added or edited with a `purchaseUrl`, its domain is matched to an existing household Retailer (or one is auto-created with a guessed name), the bottle is stamped with the `retailerId`, and the URL is written through as the (supplement, retailer) saved link — most recent purchase URL wins. So retailer history, average prices, and Check Site links populate as a side effect of normal bottle logging, and the planner arrives pre-filled. (Originally specified as a one-time backfill; there was no data yet, so the forward wiring replaced it.)

## Consequences

- **Prices are session-scoped and per-bottle sticker only.** Entered prices die when an item is purchased or removed (stale prices are worse than none); shipping and tax are never itemized — shipping exists only as the threshold gap, so an under-threshold order shows the gap but adds no fee to totals. Durable price truth stays where it always was: `bottle.price` at purchase time.
- **Retailer orders are derived, never stored.** Selecting an offer just updates the item; grouping, subtotals, and threshold status recompute reactively. There is no order entity to keep consistent.
- **Purchasing qty N creates N bottle rows at the confirmed price/count**, preserving the per-bottle FIFO ledger (ADR-0002) and its cost math unchanged; re-anchoring and forecast refresh come free from the existing add path.
- **Two knobs only**, both household-level: forecast window (30d, urgency signalling) and **coverage target** (90d, drives recommended quantity `max(1, ceil((rate × coverageDays − onHand) / bottleCount))`).
- **Retailers are managed inline and can't be deleted in v1** (rename/edit only; deletion would dangle bottle history and saved links — archive later if clutter demands).
- `FUTURE_FEATURES.md`'s "Buy: Price Comparison Page" section is superseded by this decision.
